Adulthood obesity is a serious problem among three countries

What is obesity?

According to the World Health Organization, obesity is the one's fat accumulation over body mass index (BMI) 30 that poses a health risk to humans.

Interesting discovery

Our World in Data shows that from 1975 to 2016, Nauru, an island country in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, has the highest number of adults who are obese. It increases from 44.5% of adult population in 1975 to 61% of that in 2016. For Cook Islands and Palau, both of them have a rapid increasing rate from around 28% in 1975 to around 59% in 2016. And since 2001, Cook Islands's proportion of obese adults has topped that of Palau.
